About Me

Hello there! I'm Rishi Ghia, currently navigating through an exciting academic journey at the University of Pennsylvania, where I'm pursuing my Master’s in Computer and Information Science, set to graduate in May 2024. Over the past year, I've delved deep into the intricacies of Machine Learning, Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, and Deep Learning, gaining both theoretical knowledge and practical application through a mix of coursework, research, projects, and internships.
My journey began with a Bachelor’s in Electronics Engineering from the University of Mumbai, where I laid the groundwork for my technical prowess. Now, at Penn, I am not just expanding my horizons but also applying my learning in real-world scenarios, like pioneering a GPT-driven tutoring platform and implementing advanced sentiment analysis. My current role as a Graduate Research Assistant at the World Well-Being Project allows me to explore the fascinating intersection of NLP tools and individualized learning experiences.
I have honed my programming skills across several languages, including Python, Java, C, and C++, and developed proficiency in using libraries and tools like TensorFlow, PyTorch, OpenCV, and YOLO. My technical acumen is broad, encompassing cloud platforms like AWS and GCP, and various software including Docker, Kubernetes, and Linux.
I’m actively exploring new graduate opportunities for 2024, particularly in the fields of Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Computer Vision, Machine Perception, and NLP. While these areas are my main focus, my versatile skill set and hands-on experience in various projects and technologies also position me as a strong candidate for Software Development roles. I’m eager to bring my passion for technology and innovation to a forward-thinking organization, contribute meaningfully to projects, and continue my learning journey in the ever-evolving field of Computer Science.
